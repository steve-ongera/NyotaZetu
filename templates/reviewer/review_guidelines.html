Review Guidelines

Best practices and standards for reviewing bursary applications

Start Reviewing

Introduction

As a ward-level reviewer, you play a crucial role in ensuring that bursary funds reach the most deserving students in your community. This guide provides standardized criteria and procedures for reviewing applications fairly and consistently.

Review Criteria

Need Assessment (0-10 points)

Consider the following factors:

  • Household monthly income
  • Number of siblings in school
  • Family size and dependents
  • Parent/guardian employment status
  • Outstanding fee balance

Scoring Guide:

  • 9-10: Extreme financial need
  • 7-8: High financial need
  • 5-6: Moderate need
  • 3-4: Low need
  • 0-2: Minimal need

Merit/Academic Performance (0-10 points)

Evaluate academic achievement:

  • Previous year's academic performance
  • Attendance record
  • Teacher recommendations
  • Demonstrated effort and improvement

Scoring Guide:

  • 9-10: Excellent (80%+ average)
  • 7-8: Very Good (70-79%)
  • 5-6: Good (60-69%)
  • 3-4: Fair (50-59%)
  • 0-2: Below average (<50%)

Vulnerability Assessment (0-10 points)

Special circumstances:

  • Orphan status (partial or total)
  • Disability or special needs
  • Chronic illness (student or guardian)
  • Single parent household
  • Child-headed household

Scoring Guide:

  • 10: Total orphan with disability
  • 8-9: Total orphan or severe disability
  • 6-7: Partial orphan or chronic illness
  • 4-5: Single parent or one special need
  • 0-3: No special vulnerabilities

Review Process

1

Document Verification

Verify that all required documents are present and authentic:

  • National ID card or birth certificate
  • Admission letter
  • Fee structure and balance statement
  • Academic results/transcripts
  • Parent/guardian ID copies
  • Chief's recommendation letter
2

Application Review

Carefully review all information provided:

  • Check for completeness and accuracy
  • Verify family and financial details
  • Review guardian employment status
  • Assess household income claims
  • Note any inconsistencies
3

Scoring Assessment

Apply the scoring criteria objectively:

  • Rate financial need (0-10)
  • Rate academic merit (0-10)
  • Rate vulnerability factors (0-10)
  • Calculate total score (0-30)
  • Consider priority ranking
4

Amount Recommendation

Determine appropriate bursary amount based on:

  • Total score from assessment
  • Outstanding fee balance
  • Available ward allocation
  • Category guidelines (min/max amounts)
  • Previous allocations received
5

Write Review Comments

Document your assessment clearly:

  • Explain your recommendation
  • Note key factors influencing decision
  • Highlight any concerns or red flags
  • Suggest amount and justification
  • Be professional and objective
6

Submit Recommendation

Choose appropriate action:

  • Approve: Recommend for funding
  • Reject: Does not meet criteria
  • More Info: Requires additional documents
  • Forward: Send to next review level

Best Practices

DO

  • Review applications within 7 days of submission
  • Be consistent in applying criteria
  • Document your reasoning clearly
  • Verify critical information when in doubt
  • Consider the whole application holistically
  • Recommend amounts within category limits
  • Maintain confidentiality of applicant information
  • Treat all applicants fairly and equally

DON'T

  • Let personal relationships influence decisions
  • Discriminate based on gender, religion, or ethnicity
  • Share applicant details with unauthorized persons
  • Rush through reviews without proper assessment
  • Recommend amounts beyond available allocation
  • Ignore supporting documents or verification
  • Use offensive or unprofessional language
  • Delay reviews unnecessarily

Common Issues & Solutions

What if documents are missing?
Select "Request More Information" and clearly specify which documents are needed. Do not reject immediately - give applicants 7 days to provide missing documents.
How to handle conflicting information?
Document the inconsistencies in your comments and recommend additional verification. You may request clarification from the applicant or forward to constituency level for investigation.
What if ward allocation is insufficient?
Prioritize applicants with highest need scores. Forward exceptional cases to constituency level with strong justification for additional allocation consideration.
How to handle duplicate applications?
Check if applicant received bursary in previous rounds. Note this in comments and recommend accordingly - repeat applicants may receive lower priority unless circumstances changed.

Ethical Standards

As a reviewer, you must uphold the highest standards of integrity:

Confidentiality

All applicant information is confidential. Never share details with unauthorized persons or use for purposes outside the bursary program.

Impartiality

Review all applications objectively without bias. Recuse yourself from reviewing applications where you have a conflict of interest.

Zero Tolerance for Corruption

Never solicit or accept bribes, gifts, or favors in exchange for recommendations. Report any corruption attempts immediately.

Accountability

Your decisions affect students' educational futures. Be prepared to justify your recommendations with documented evidence.
